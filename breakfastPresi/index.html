Traditional Research Synthesis

Pros

  • Systematic reviews and meta-analyses as gold standards
  • Often become seminal papers in their fields
  • Very specific research questions
  • Rigorous methodological frameworks
  • Systematic data collection

Cons

  • Limited by human processing capacity
  • Struggles with large corpora of literature
  • Time and resource intensive
  • Difficulty capturing multi-disciplinary connections
  • Does not consider the evolution of a topic

Computational approaches offer complementary opportunities as literature volume expands

Traditional approaches remain valuable but face scaling challenges as the volume of scientific literature grows exponentially.

Computational Research Synthesis

  • Novel computational tools emerging
  • Connected Papers, Semantic Scholar, VOSviewer
  • Enabling large-scale literature exploration

Case Study: SSRIs

  • Most widely prescribed antidepressant class
  • Profound societal impact since the 1980s
  • Extensive research spanning decades
  • High prevalence in clinical practice

Multidisciplinary Perspectives

  • Neuroscience: Mechanism of action and neural effects
  • Psychiatry: Clinical efficacy and patient outcomes
  • Philosophy: Mind - Body problem
  • Environmental Science: Wastewater treatment

Beyond Traditional Reviews

Traditional literature reviews cannot adequately capture:

  • The full scope of SSRI research
  • Complex interdisciplinary connections
  • Temporal evolution of knowledge
  • Emergent research clusters and communities

Bro, we all came for the regulatory science?

  • How does knowledge develop after marketing authorization?
  • How long does it take for safety signals to appear?
  • How do regulatory decisions influence research directions?
  • How are new indications of approved drugs 'discovered'?

Study Objectives

Mapping the SSRI knowledge landscape to reveal:

  • Historical evolution of research themes
  • Interconnections between pharmacological and clinical studies
  • Knowledge development patterns post-marketing
  • Disciplinary contributions and blind spots

Methods

  1. Fetch articles from Scopus
  2. Fetch reference data
  3. Embed title and abstract using an LLM
  4. Finding most similar papers using nearest neighbor and cosine similarity
  5. Create network based on citations and semantic similarity
  6. Cluster the network based on its connectedness
  7. Label clusters qualitatively based on most central papers
  8. Hierarchical organization of clusters
  9. Analyse clusters
  10. Visualize clusters interactively
